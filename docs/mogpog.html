About Mogpog

Mogpog, officially the Municipality of Mogpog, (Tagalog: Bayan ng Mogpog) is a 3rd class municipality in the province of Marinduque, Philippines. According to the 2015 census, it has a population of 34,043 people. The Primary Gateway of the Province through the Balanacan Port. It is the original home of the Moriones Lenten Rites and Kangga Festiva. Mogpog was named after the Tagalog word, mag-aapog or kiln makers abundant in the municipality

History

In 1942, the Japanese troops occupied the town of Mogpog,Marinduque.In 1945, in the Second World War the liberation of the town of Mogpog, Marinduque, the American and Filipino troops fought against the Japanese Imperial forces during the Battle of Marinduque.Historically the famous Moriones Festival is said to have originated from Mogpog. Moriones Festival was founded by a Spanish friar Rev.Father Dionisio Santiago the first parish priest of mogpog. This festival is known to be one of the most colorful festivals in Marinduque and the Philippines. It is held in Mogpog and the surrounding areas of Marinduque island.

Luzon Datum

This rock marker was established in 1911 by the US Coast and Geodetic Survey as the Geodetic Ceneter of the country.It features a hole drilled inside an etched triangle in diorute rock outcrop at the top of the highest hill(272.42 MASL) in Brgy. Hinanggayon.The provincial government of Marinduque,DENR,NAMRIA developed the area and was inaugurated in 2011.It is located almost 360-degree view of Tayabas Bay, Mompong Pass, and Tablas Strait.It is managed and maintained by the local folks as Community-Based Eco-Tourism Program.

Natangco White Beach

A protected marine sanctuary, Natangco Island is part of Barangay Silangan, Mogpog, Marinduque.Surrounded by 30 to 40 feet deep fringing reefs excellent for diving amd snorkeling. The island can be reached by rentd boats from Barangay Ino and Barangay Balanacan.The island boasts a strip of ehite sand beach, pristine and clear waters and rocky island formations off its coaost.
